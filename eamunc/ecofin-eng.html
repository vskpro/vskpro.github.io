Economic and Financial Affairs Council

Economic and Financial Affairs Council

The Economic and Financial Committee is one of six committees that constitute the United Nations General Assembly. The Economic and Financial Committee, otherwise known as the Second Committee of the General Assembly or ECOFIN, handles issues relating to development, financial sustainability and economic growth and development with specific regard to macroeconomic policy on international trade and external debt sustainability, securing financing for sustainable development, poverty eradication and globalization between states. ECOFIN serves as one of the primary forums for the members of the United Nations to tackle the global financial issues that face the global economy.

Letter from the Secretary General

Dear Delegates,

Welcome to the EA MUNC 2018! I am Somalika Chhabra and I will be serving as the chair of the Economic and Finance Committee. MUN has given me a platform and has boosted my confidence in a lot of ways. MUN is not only about debating on the topics provided, but, sharing resources with the aim for getting solutions to current issues, it’s about team work and accepting different views and opinions and last but not the least interacting and meeting different individuals. Along with Vice Chair, Suchi Smita Singh and Rapporteur, Merin Sebastian, I hope that this MUN plays the same role of providing a whole new platform to you as it has given to me.

Technology is crucial in empowering people to participate in our digital future, and in helping governments to better serve people. Access to the Internet is the main operational issue on sustainable development and the Internet. Technical infrastructure is necessary but is not a sufficient condition for full access to the Internet.In today's world, the reality is that all individuals and organisations connected to the internet are vulnerable to cyber-attack. It's not only large organisations that are under threat. Individuals or organisations that don't believe they have much to offer hackers can still be targeted. This affects the efficiency and cyber security of individuals as well as of an economy. Improving risk management and cyber security and creating a sustainable cyber security workforce by changing the long-term patterns of an enterprise and creating an organizational culture of cyber security are critical to improving security in a sustainable manner. We, as a committee will strive on coming up with a resolution to help the development of the member states.

My advice to all delegate is “Don’t hold yourself back, express your views and opinions freely. You might not know, which remark of yours becomes an essential solution”. And if you need any assistance, do not hesitate to approach the bureau.

I look forward to meeting you all and I hope you all enjoy this conference just as much as we have in preparing and conducting it for you.

Somalika Chhabra
Chair, ECOFIN

Topic

Cyber Crime as a Hindrance to Economic Development


The threat from cyber crime is multi-dimensional, targeting citizens, businesses, and governments at a rapidly growing rate. Cyber criminal tools pose a direct threat to security and play an increasingly important role in facilitating most forms of organized crime and terrorism.

The first job of a government is to protect the life, liberty, property and privacy of its citizens. In recent years, cyber crime has become one of the most pervasive global phenomena, raising the concerns of governments, citizens and business enterprises. During the next five years, cyber crime might become the greatest threat to every person, place and thing in the world. Thus, tackling cybercrime must be at the heart of every government’s agenda.
